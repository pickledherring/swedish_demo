Over a century of Swedish song in Seattle

The Swedish Women's Chorus (founded in 1951) and the Svea Male Chorus (founded in 1905) joined in 2017 to form the Swedish Singers of Seattle. As a Swedish heritage choir, our mission is to preserve and promote Swedish choral music. We are proud members of the American Union of Swedish Singers and of the Greater Seattle Choral Consortium.

1905 - Svea Manskor Founded

The oldest active Swedish heritage chorus on the West Coast is created from the merging of Svenska Klubbens Sangkor and Svenska Gleeklubben. Svea Manksor has also gone by Svea Men's Chorus and the Svea Male Chorus.

1951 - Swedish Women's Chorus Founded

The first American chorus for women only devoted to primarily singing in Swedish is founded. The Women's Chorus sings for local Swedish events and travels as far as Canada and Connecticut. In 1968, they tour Sweden and Finland, and in 1980, Sweden and Iceland.

2017 - Swedish Singers Founded

After the departure of longtime director Allen Andrews, Svea Men's Chorus and the Swedish Women's Chorus combine ranks to form the Swedish Singers of Seattle. Director Dan White and a choir of around 15 singers provide music for cultural events at the Swedish Club in Seattle and surrounding areas.
